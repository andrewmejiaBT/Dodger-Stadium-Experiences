First Impressions

I remember when I had first stepped foot into Dodger Stadium, many years ago, and I was amazed. The stadium felt small on television when I would watch the Los Dodgers take the field, but in person it felt like you were walking into a castle. For a little boy who was 11 years old at the time, seeing so many people gathered to watch two teams slug it out with bats and gloves was something I will never forget.

NLCS Game

Going to my very first National League Championship game at Dodger Stadium, I had somewhat of an idea of what to expect. Dodger dogs passed along, beer in a Dodger cup barely spilt, and some people wearing the opposing teams' colors/jerseys. What I did not expect was the sheer loudness of the crowd, as it was so deafening I could not hear my Dad yelling my name when the Dodgers hit a home run! I jumped out my seat when I realized my favorite baseball had just scored, winning us the game and my team moving onto the playoffs. I will always thank my sister and my father for inviting me that day.

Bus Rides

Taking the bus to Dodger Stadium is tough, since you are standing up for at least 40 minutes, if not more. Factor in the wait time for each stop and it takes the streets through Downtown LA, you have a recipe for sore calves. The buses are usually packed as well, with each person being about a mere foot apart standing. Most of the seats are taken by the time you board the bus, but it beats having to $25 for parking and driving back home late.

About Dodger Stadium

Dodger Stadium is home to the Los Angeles Dodgers, located in the Elysian Park neighborhood of Los Angeles, California. It first opened in 1962, when the original Brooklyn Dodgers moved to Los Angeles. It has a maximum capacity of 56,000 and costed $23 million USD (inflation adjusted: $197 million USD) to construct for Opening Day.
